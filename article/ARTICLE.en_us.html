Lavasciuga is a unicase rounded sans-serif typeface inspired by the hand-painted lettering found on small retail storefronts across Italy in the 1980s and 1990s — laundromats, gelaterias, tire dealers — where signage was friendly, direct, and made to be read at a glance.

The design is built around a single optical height shared across all glyphs, with regular proportions and minimal width contrast. Diacritics, descenders, and extended marks break this baseline only when necessary. Punctuation and quotation marks stay contained within the cap height, reinforcing the compact, sign-like texture of the typeface. Overlapping components — such as double-T combinations — are fully flattened, making Lavasciuga suitable for outline and layered type treatments without artifacts.

To contribute, see github.com/lucabessiaristei/lavasciuga

875 glyphs across three scripts

Lavasciuga covers extended Latin, extended Cyrillic, and basic Greek. The Latin set spans Western and Central European languages, South Eastern European, South American, Oceanian, and Esperanto. Cyrillic covers Belarusian, Russian, Bulgarian, Serbian, Macedonian, Ukrainian, and Adobe Extended. The unicase structure — a single unified optical height rather than separate uppercase and lowercase — carries consistently across all three writing systems.

OpenType features

The typeface includes fractions, superscripts, subscripts, ordinals, and numerator and denominator figures via frac, sups, subs, numr, dnom, and ordn. Nine currency symbols are included: dollar, euro, sterling, yen, cent, ruble, rupee, shekel, and won.

Design details

Curves are soft and deliberate, tuned to suggest a consistent stroke weight throughout without mechanical rigidity. Spacing is intentionally tight, echoing the dense, efficient lettering of hand-painted commercial signs. The absence of stylistic alternates is a design decision: Lavasciuga is a single-voice typeface, structured and unambiguous, the way a good storefront sign should be.

Lavasciuga was designed by Luca Bessi Aristei.
