A unicase rounded sans-serif typeface inspired by the approachable lettering found on small retail storefronts of the 1980s and 1990s.

To preserve its identity as a signage typeface, I designed it with a single optical height across all glyphs, favoring highly regular proportions and minimal width contrast. Diacritics, descenders, and extended marks break this height only when necessary, while punctuation and quotation marks remain contained within the cap height.

Aiming to create an accessible and versatile typeface, I developed Lavasciuga with extended Latin, extended Cyrillic, and basic Greek character sets, with the unicase structure carrying consistently across all three writing systems.

The curves are soft and controlled, carefully crafted to convey a consistent stroke weight throughout. Spacing is deliberately tight, helping fill any laundromat or gelateria sign. All overlapping components have been flattened, allowing the typeface to work seamlessly in outline styles.

The result is a friendly but structured typeface that reminds me of the small urban landscapes of Italy as I remember them from childhood.

OpenType features

Lavasciuga includes fractions, superscripts, subscripts, ordinals, and numerator and denominator figures through frac, sups, subs, numr, dnom, and ordn, alongside nine currency symbols: dollar, euro, sterling, yen, cent, ruble, rupee, shekel, and won.
